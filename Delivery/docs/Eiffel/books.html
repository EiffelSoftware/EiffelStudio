Books and other documentation on Eiffel

All of these books are available from ISE; for ordering, please e-mail <info@eiffel.com>. The Prentice Hall books are also available from Prentice Hall and from technical bookstores.

"Object-Oriented Software Construction 2nd Edition", Bertrand Meyer
This book is a comprehensive reference on all aspects of object technology, from design principles to O-O techniques, Design by Contract, O-O analysis, concurrency, persistence, abstract data types and many more. Written by a pioneer in the field, contains an in-depth analysis of both methodological and technical issues.

Two-color printing provides for clear figures and readable software extracts.

"Object Succes", Bertrand Meyer
A Manager's Guide to Object Orientation, its impact on the corporation, and its use for reengineering the software process.
This is a book meant for managers. It presents the technology in a simple way, but mostly concentrates on business, organizational and economic issues.
Prentice Hall, 1995
ISBN 0-13-192833-3

"Object-Oriented Applications", Bertrand Meyer and Jean-Marc Nerson (eds.)
Chapters written by authors from companies in Canada, Europe and Australia describe in detail seven Eiffel projects in areas such as telecommunications, CAD-CAM or AI, and discuss the lessons that can be drawn by other teams.
ISBN 0-13--13798-7.

"Eiffel: The Language", Bertrand Meyer
The official language description. Prentice Hall, second printing, 1992.
ISBN 0-13-247925-7.

"Reusable Software: The Base Object-Oriented Component Libraries", Bertrand Meyer
An in-depth examination of the principles of building reusable software, accompanied by a detailed description of EiffelBase, EiffelLex and EiffelParse. Prentice Hall, 1994.
ISBN: 0-13-245499-8.

"An Object-Oriented Environment: Principles and Application", Bertrand Meyer
Discusses what it means for an environment to be object-oriented and presents EiffelBench in detail. Also includes an introduction to EiffelBuild. Prentice Hall, 1994.
ISBN 0-13-245507-2.

"Eiffel: An Introduction", Robert Switzer
A concise introduction to Eiffel. Prentice Hall, 1993.
ISBN: 0-13-105909-2.

"Seamless Object-Oriented Software Architecture - Analysis and Design of Reliable Systems", Kim Walden and Jean-Marc Nerson
A discussion of analysis and design emphasizing seamlessness, reversibility and Design by Contract, and describing the BON method (Business Object Notation). Prentice Hall, 1995.
ISBN 0-13-031303-3

"An Eiffel Collection", Bertrand Meyer
A collection, published by ISE, of articles about Eiffel, some hard to find.
